Understanding Mental Health

Knowledge is the first step toward healing

üò∞

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders involve excessive fear, worry, or nervousness that can interfere with daily activities. Common types include generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, and social anxiety disorder.

Common Symptoms:

  • Excessive worry or fear
  • Restlessness or feeling on edge
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Physical symptoms (rapid heartbeat, sweating)
  • Sleep disturbances
Affects ~31% of adults
üòî

Depression

Depression is a mood disorder that causes persistent feelings of sadness, loss of interest, and can affect how you think, feel, and handle daily activities.

Common Symptoms:

  • Persistent sad or empty mood
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Fatigue or loss of energy
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Difficulty making decisions
Affects ~21% of adults
üîÑ

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder causes unusual shifts in mood, energy, activity levels, and the ability to carry out day-to-day tasks. It includes periods of mania and depression.

Common Symptoms:

  • Mood swings (highs and lows)
  • Changes in energy levels
  • Impulsive behavior during manic episodes
  • Depressive episodes
  • Sleep pattern changes
Affects ~2.8% of adults
üß©

PTSD

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. It can cause flashbacks, nightmares, and severe anxiety.

Common Symptoms:

  • Flashbacks or nightmares
  • Avoidance of trauma reminders
  • Negative thoughts and mood
  • Hypervigilance
  • Sleep disturbances
Affects ~6.8% of adults

Eating Disorders

Eating disorders are serious mental health conditions characterized by unhealthy eating behaviors and preoccupation with food, body weight, and shape.

Common Symptoms:

  • Extreme restriction of food intake
  • Binge eating episodes
  • Purging behaviors
  • Preoccupation with body image
  • Distorted body perception
Affects ~9% of population
üßò

OCD

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder involves unwanted, intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions) that interfere with daily life.

Common Symptoms:

  • Obsessive thoughts or fears
  • Compulsive behaviors or rituals
  • Difficulty controlling thoughts
  • Time-consuming rituals
  • Distress when rituals are prevented
Affects ~2.3% of adults
